How To Cut Watermelon Into Sticks





Cutting your watermelon into sticks vs triangles can help avoid trying to eat watermelon with juice running down your face. Sticks can also be stored easier in a container. Cutting sticks can be done in just a few easy steps and these steps can be applied to other fruits too!










Instructions

Step One

Wash your watermelon with cool water to get rid of any dirt or pesticides. If you prefer to remove the rind you can but it serves as a good holding point for your sticks. I suggest to keep the rind on.

Step Two

Cut your watermelon in half on a large cutting board. You want to cut along the width of the watermelon (not through the stems).

Step Three

Place your watermelon cut down, on the cutting board. Begin Slicing your watermelon into 1" to 1.5" slices in one direction. It doesnt matter which direction you start with.

Step Four

Carefully turn your watermelon, or move yourself, and start cutting in the opposite direction. Try to keep your cuts the same width. This will keep your sticks uniform in size and shape.

Step Five

Gently start to pull each stick out form the rind. It's that simple! Next you will repeat steps 1-4 on the other half of the watermelon. P.S.You can freeze these if you want a cold treat too!

Bonus Tip!

Choosing A Ripe Watermelon:

  • Look for a firm watermelon without bruises or dents. Superficial scratches are fine but there shouldn't be any soft spots on the melon.
  • The watermelon should feel heavy. Watermelon is 92% water so it should feel hefty for its size.
  • Check out the bottom of the melon and look for a smooth yellow/creamy spot. This is where it sat on the ground and ripened in the sun on the vine.
